[intro]
As you walk the farm road, a stray dog begins to follow you.
That is warning enough. If there is one, there may be more. You turn and try to scare it off. It retreats, but not far, and still keeps to your trail.

[success]
By a broken fence, you find a stout stick and a scatter of stones. Good. If there are more, you will not meet them empty-handed.

Then you see them: shapes under the trees on the far side of the road, dogs or half-wild wolves, hard to tell in the dimness. The stray presses close against your leg, whining softly, and you understand at once. It is not hunting you. It is asking shelter from the pack in the woods.

Better two than one. You stop, wait, and let them come. When the ring closes, you hold until the leader steps nearest, then drive a stone straight into its snout. After that it is noise, wood, teeth, and quick footwork, but you hold your ground. At last the pack breaks and slinks back into the trees.

When the road is quiet again, $companionName stays beside you as though the choice has already been made.

[failure]
If there is one, there may be more, and you decide not to risk the road finding out how many.
You snatch up a stone and throw it at the stray. It yelps and runs off, and for a while that feels like wisdom. Later, when the trees begin to move with distant shapes, you are no longer so sure.
