[intro]
You sit on a fence rail, smoking tobacco and watching the road, when a man comes up the lane with a mule at the end of a rope.
There is something wrong in the way they move together. The mule halts, and the man answers with the stick in his hand.

[success]
The mule stops. The man strikes it. It stops again, and he strikes it once more.

That is enough for you. You hop down from the fence and call out, "Sorry, but that is not right."
"What is not right?" he snaps.
"Hitting the mule like that. Stop it."
"None of your business."
"I'm making it my business."

He turns and swings the stick at you instead, catching you once before you rush him. After that it is short and ugly. You hit him low, drive him down into the roadside dust, and pin him there before he can bring the stick around again.
"Now hit me with it," you say.
He does not. He only whimpers that he should have left the brute where it stood. Then, half in anger and half in fear, he spits out that you can take the mule if you care so much.

When you let him go, he stumbles off muttering under his breath. The old mule only looks at you, long and level, as if measuring whether your anger was the right kind. At last, $companionName steps over to your side.

[failure]
You watch the man strike the mule and finally shout down from the fence, "Hey, stop that."
"Stop what?" he calls back.
"Hitting the mule."
"Then it should walk."

He does stop hitting it, but only to drag it by the rope instead. After a while the pair move on down the lane, slower than before and no better off. You tell yourself at least you interrupted the cruelty, but the thought sits poorly with you once the road is empty again.
