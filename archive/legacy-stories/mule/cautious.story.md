@title A safe companion

[intro]
You have learned that the safest road through the countryside is often a shared one.
So near the end of market day, you wait by the square and ask whether anyone is traveling your way. At last one trader nods and says you may come along, if you will help him pack the wagon first.

[success]
The mule draws the wagon steadily as the two of you make your way up the road.
Then, all at once, it stops dead.

You look up and see why. Men are stepping out of the brush ahead, too many to mistake for honest travelers. Robbers.

The mule saw them first and chose wisely. You waste no time. You haul the trader up onto the mule's back, turn the beast hard, and send them trotting back toward town.

The robbers come at you instead, but you are quick enough on your feet to keep your distance. You give ground, shout mockery at them, and make them chase you just long enough for mule and master to escape.

When at last you circle back, the trader is waiting where the safer road begins, pale and shaken and laughing with relief.
"You saved me a fortune today," he says.

The mule comes over then and presses its head lightly against your shoulder. The trader smiles.
"That one likes you," the old man says. "More than it likes me, I think."
And from then on, $companionName keeps your road.

[failure]
You leap onto the cart for higher ground, hoping to turn fear into advantage.
But you are a moment too late. The trader is already beaten down by numbers and hurried into handing over his savings. In the confusion you jump clear, skid down the roadside slope, and vanish into the bushes with nothing to show for it but the sour taste of bad timing.
