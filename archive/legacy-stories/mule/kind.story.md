@title One song for a Mule

[intro]
You are singing to yourself and filling your pipe with tobacco when you hear hooves coming unevenly up behind you.
When you turn, you see a mule limping badly, harness dragging, with no owner anywhere in sight.

[success]
The poor thing is wild-eyed with pain. You move slowly, speaking low until it lets you come near, then slip an arm around its neck and wait for the panic to ease.

Only when its breathing has steadied do you bend to the injured leg. There, half-buried in the hoof, is the cause: a long nail driven cruelly into the tender part.

You work it free as gently as you can. Then you gather plantain from the roadside, crush the leaves in your palm, and press the cool green mash into the wound before leading the mule onto softer ground.

It stays with you after that, limping but calmer, as though it has already decided the matter. By evening, $companionName follows at your shoulder with the deep trust of a beast that has been handled kindly at the right hour.

[failure]
It is a mule, limping badly, and pity catches you at once.
But you only watch it pass, hurt and riderless, and tell yourself there must be someone else to care for it farther down the road. The sight of it stays with you longer than you would like.
