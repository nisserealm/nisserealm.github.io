@title Donkey

[intro]
Just as the road bends into a thicket, you see a herdsman lying in the ditch.
You rush to him. The herdsman is alive, barely, whispering only, "My herd... my herd..."
You drag him clear of the road, lay him on his side, and then head in the direction his shaking hand points.

[success]
You find them not far ahead: three thieves trying to drive the herd off into the woods.
You cut through cover, circle wide, and hit the hindmost one before the thief even knows you are there, driving him flat into the ground.

"Call them off," you whisper, "or this is where it ends for you."
The thief believes you. That is enough. The others curse, hesitate, and finally choose their companion over the stolen herd.

You make the fallen man help turn the animals back toward the road before you let him go. That night you stay beside the injured herdsman and help him keep watch.

Near dawn, one donkey comes over to you and makes a rough, laughing sound that only a donkey could. The old herdsman hears it and mutters, half amused, "That one likes you."

[failure]
You see them up the road and shout your anger before you are close enough to do anything useful.
They turn on you at once. The next thing you remember is the old herdsman dragging you half-conscious from the ditch.
"Brave enough," he mutters. "Not bright enough."
