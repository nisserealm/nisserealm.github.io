[intro]
Sitting beneath a shaded tree, you notice a few men coming along the road who look badly out of place.
You stay hidden and watch. They speak in low voices, then slip into cover just as a herder comes down the road with his animals. An ambush is taking shape.
[success]
You creep closer and wait until they spring.
Then you come crashing down on them from behind with all the noise and fury you can muster. It is two against three, until one of the donkeys joins in as well, lashing out and biting one of the robbers hard enough to send the whole attack into confusion.

That is enough. The thieves break and scatter. You spend the night helping the shaken herdsman watch over his animals.

Near the fire, one donkey comes over and stands beside you as if the choice is obvious. The old herdsman gives you a dry look and says, "That one likes you."

[failure]
You see the robbers preparing their ambush and decide not to meet them head-on.
Instead you make loud noises, as if speaking to companions just behind you. The thieves startle, break cover too early, and scatter.

It is enough to save yourself, but not enough to truly stop them. You slip into the forest before their anger can settle on you.
