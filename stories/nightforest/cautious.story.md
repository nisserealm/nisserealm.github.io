# Eyes Above the Path

Night has already gathered under the branches by the time you enter the deeper wood.
You do not trust your sight alone there, so you walk by sound: leaf-fall, bark-creak, the brief panicked scurry of little lives that do not yet know what you are.

At last you hear it: two soft wingbeats, then a pause, then the same two wingbeats again from somewhere above and to your left.

You stop beneath an old hollow tree and wait without speaking.
After a while a shape slips down from the dark branch above you, pale-eyed and silent, its head turning once, then twice, as if measuring your worth.

You see the trouble then. A strip of old cord is wound around one leg, biting into the feathers and flesh where the creature must have fought to free itself.

You do not drag it close or try to master it.
You crush broad night leaves between your fingers until their bitter juice runs, and with slow care you work the cord loose and press the green wetness into the wound.

The owl watches every motion, but does not strike.

Instead it follows above you from branch to branch, keeping your road under its silent guard.
