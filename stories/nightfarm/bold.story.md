# The Mule in Wolf-Time

The lane drops you near sleeping fields just as shouting breaks out somewhere ahead.
Lantern light jerks wildly in the dark, and hooves strike the earth with the hard sound of panic.

You stride in without waiting to be asked.

A mule has gone wild with fear after wolves came too close to a sheep fold on the hillside.
It has snapped one line and is dragging a rough timber sledge behind it, the runners striking sparks from stone whenever it wheels around.

No one dares get near enough to stop it.
You seize the dragging rope as it sweeps past, plant your feet, and wrench the sledge sideways into the mud so the mule cannot keep bolting in circles.
The beast rears so close you feel the wind of it, but you do not let go.

When at last it stands blowing hard instead of fighting, you press dock leaf and marsh-mallow root into the rope-burned place at its chest where the harness cut deep.
The mule watches you with hot, furious eyes until the fear runs out of them.

Its owner, shaken and half ashamed, says the beast has been near broken since winter and has no more use for wolf-fright in it.
So when you say you will take it, he only nods.
