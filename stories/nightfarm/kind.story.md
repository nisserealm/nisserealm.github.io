# The Little Grey at the Shrine

The cold of the late hours settles hardest in the low places where damp gathers.
You follow a little path between sleeping fields and come at last to a roadside shrine beneath an ash tree, with a spring trickling darkly nearby.

A little lamp burns there, nearly out.
You set it right, speak kindly to whatever hears you, and listen for the small frightened movement hidden just beyond the stonework.

There, half sheltered by the shrine wall, stands a little grey donkey shivering in the damp.
Someone has left it tied with too short a cord beside the spring, perhaps meaning to return and perhaps never having meant to.

You work the wet rope loose, rub warmth into its ears and neck, and lead it to the spring so it can drink properly instead of straining at the stone lip.
Then you gather dry reeds from the bank and heap them where the ground is least cold.

It follows you when you move, first only with its eyes and then with a step, then another, until it is nosing the fold of your cloak as though warmth itself had taken a shape it can trust.
