# Reindeer

Weather is gathering into a hard northern storm, but you welcome the wind and press on with a quicker step.
Then you notice the tracks by the riverbank: wolf prints, fresh and deep. Stray or pack, you cannot yet tell. Curiosity takes hold of you, and you follow them around the bend until the whole scene opens at once.

Only a lone wolf. You do not stop to measure the danger, but charge as if it were your quarry and not the other way around.
The beast turns in surprise, falters, then skids off into the reeds. The reindeer lifts her head and lets out a strange low sound, almost like thanks. A moment later, $companionName comes lightly to your side as if boldness itself had called her there.
