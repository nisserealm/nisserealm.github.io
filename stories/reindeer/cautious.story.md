# Reindeer

Crossing the high plateau, you feel the mist gathering before you fully see it. Moisture thickens in the air, and the farther ridges begin to fade.
Best to play this one safely. You remember a reindeer herder you passed not long ago, and turn back before the fog makes even the most trodden paths uncertain.

At last you find them: reindeer drawn into a close ring, the herder just beyond them, half-lost in the whitening air.
You call out before coming too near. The old herder is plainly relieved to see another soul in the mist. A smaller group has gone missing, he tells you, and he cannot leave the gathered herd unattended. You offer to stay.

So you spend the evening among the reindeer, keeping them calm with song and low talk while the fog presses close around the camp. Once or twice you almost think they answer you. When the herder finally returns with the lost animals, he studies you with new respect.
