# Reindeer

You love the open quiet of the highlands. As you walk, you pick cranberries and sing to yourself.
You only know one song, so you vary it as best you can, humming here and there, then beginning again from the start.

Then, all at once, it feels as though another voice has tuned itself to yours.
You stop and listen. Something is moving nearby. A wolf comes into view, but it is already backing away, reluctant, disturbed by your presence. It circles off slowly, always watching.

When you step closer, you find the reason: a reindeer half-hidden in the brush, shivering, one leg torn by a bite and too frightened to rise.
You begin your song again, softer now, and kneel beside her. There is nothing to do but make camp where you are. So you build a small fire and keep watch beside the reindeer until her trembling eases and sleep finally takes her.
