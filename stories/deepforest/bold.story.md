# Where the Wolves Broke

The path gives way to broken brush and the smell of blood.
Ahead comes the sound of wolves in a frenzy, circling something that still refuses to die quietly.

You do not stop to weigh the wisdom of it.
You go straight toward the noise.

In a hollow between roots and stone, you find a great bear on its side, bleeding from flank and shoulder while a wolf pack worries it from every angle.

You rush in shouting, striking branches against trunks, hurling stones, making yourself seem larger and more dangerous than a lone traveler has any right to be.
When the nearest wolf turns, you meet it with such fury that the whole pack hesitates.

That heartbeat of doubt is enough.
The wolves break, one after another, melting back into the trees with snarls that promise nothing but distance.

Only then do you kneel beside the bear.
You wash its wounds, bind what you can, and stay near even when its great head lifts and its breath rolls hot across your face.
