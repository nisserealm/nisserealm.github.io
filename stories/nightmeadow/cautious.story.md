# The Sleepless Ears

You curl yourself beneath a bent meadow tree, using its roots and the long grass as what little shelter the night offers.
Sleep does not come easily.
Too much is moving out there beyond the dark line where the grass gives way to shadow.

So you keep your eyes closed and listen instead.
The meadow sorts itself slowly in your mind: the dry brushing of wind, the tiny hops of mice, the padded drift of hunting things farther off.

And then another sound.
Very soft. Very near.

When you finally open your eyes, you see a long-eared shape in the grass just beyond your reach, a little meadow rabbit holding one hind leg strangely clear of the ground.

You do not move toward it at once.
You stay where you are until the creature understands that your stillness is real.

Only then do you creep close enough to see the trouble: a thorn and burr knot worked deep into the fur and skin of its leg.
You free it with careful fingers and rub a little crushed meadow herb into the sore place, letting the cold juice ease the hurt.

The rabbit darts away a short distance, stops, and comes back before the moon has shifted much at all.
