# A Small Warm Thing

The night meadow is cold in the roots of the tree.
You draw your cloak tighter and try to settle yourself for sleep, though the dark feels too wide and the stars too clear for easy rest.

After a while you feel a faint trembling against your side.
At first you think it is only the wind in the grass, but when you look down you find a tiny hedgehog nosing into the fold of your cloak, cold enough that even its little courage has become desperate.

You laugh softly and lift the cloth just enough to let it nearer without trapping it.
There in the shelter of your arm and cloak it uncurls by slow degrees, trusting warmth before trust itself.

You share a little crumb of food and set a capful of water near the roots for it.
For a long while the two of you remain there under the tree, neither fully asleep, both less alone than the night had first intended.
