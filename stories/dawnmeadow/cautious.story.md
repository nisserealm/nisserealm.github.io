# Where the Mist Parts

The meadow is all pale breath and silver grass.
Cold lies low over the ground, and every step you take through the dawn seems loud enough to startle the whole waking world.

So you move slowly, letting the frost-dark stems brush your boots without hurry.
At the edge of the mist you notice a hare, nearly invisible until it turns its head and the first weak light catches one dark eye.

You stop at once.
The creature has one forepaw lifted, not from fear alone but from pain; a thorn has lodged there, and the cold has made it clumsy enough that it cannot work the thing loose.

You kneel in the grass and wait until the hare's trembling eases.
Then, with the smallest motion you can manage, you free the thorn and rub a little crushed clover and dew over the tender place.

The hare draws back, tests the paw, and does not flee.
Instead it circles once through the mist and comes back to you with the shy certainty of something that has made a difficult decision.
