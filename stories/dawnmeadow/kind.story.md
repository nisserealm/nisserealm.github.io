# Warm Hands at Daybreak

The dawn meadow is beautiful, but it is not gentle yet.
Frost clings to the grass heads, the light is thin, and every little creature out in the open seems to be waiting for the day to become survivable.

Near a patch of low flowers you find a songbird fallen into the cold grass, not badly hurt, only numbed by the long chill of the night.
Its wings flutter weakly when you kneel beside it, but it cannot lift itself.

You cup it in your hands without closing them, giving it warmth rather than prison.
For a while you simply breathe into your fingers and let the bird borrow the heat of you until its tiny heart no longer feels frantic with cold.

When it grows stronger, you set it in the first good light on a dry stone and crumble a few seeds there beside it.
The bird hops once, twice, then settles on your wrist as if the choice were easier than flying off at once.

It stays there longer than any frightened thing should.
