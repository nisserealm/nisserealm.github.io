# A Little Shade to Share

The hot forest feels tired.
The leaves burn pale at the edges, and even the birds sound careful with their voices, as if song itself costs too much in weather like this.

Near a fallen trunk you find a squirrel stretched in the narrow ribbon of shade, breathing fast with heat and thirst.
It is not wounded, only worn down by too many bright days and too little relief.

You kneel without reaching for it.
From your satchel you bring out a few soft berries and the last cool sip of water you can spare, letting both rest on a leaf instead of forcing your nearness on the little creature.

The squirrel watches you for a long moment, then creeps forward and drinks, paws trembling with the effort.

You break a broad green leaf from a lower branch where the tree has already half-shed it, and set it to cast a little better shade over the hollow.
For a while the two of you remain there together, sharing silence and the poor comfort the day allows.
