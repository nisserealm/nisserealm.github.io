# Dust Road Ambush

The heat has emptied the road of all but the stubborn.
Even so, somewhere beyond the next stand of thornbush comes a burst of shouting sharp enough to cut through the flies and the drowse of afternoon.

You go toward it at once.

At a bend in the backroad you find two robbers dragging a peddler from his cart while a thin old mule stands hitched to the shafts, ears flat, eyes rolling, unable to do anything but stamp and endure.

You do not pause to count the odds.
You take up a fallen branch thick as a club, rush the nearer robber, and strike hard enough to send him sprawling into the dust.
The other turns on you with a knife, but boldness breaks his nerve before steel can.
He backs away, then runs, hauling his companion after him.

Only then do you turn to the peddler and help him up.
He is bruised and frightened, but whole enough to curse the thieves and laugh a little at still being alive.
Together you right the cart and gather what has spilled into the road.

While you work, the mule keeps watching you.
When you loosen the hot leather at its neck and let it drink from the little water left in the peddler's skin, it presses its head once against your shoulder and goes on standing near you after that.

The peddler sees it and gives a tired smile.
"I was leading the poor old soul to the butcher by the river market," he says. "But after today, I think I'd sooner pay my debt to luck another way. If it wants your road, take it."
