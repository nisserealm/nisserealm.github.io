# The Mule on the Rise

You hear shouting from the chalk rise above the fields before you see what has gone wrong.
Somewhere on the backroad, where scrub and worked ground meet, something large is thrashing and men are keeping well back from it.

You head uphill at once.

There you find a mule down hard on one side beneath a broken carrying-frame, with split poles and cut wood scattered all over the road.
Two men are arguing over the loss instead of helping, each more eager to save the load than the beast.

You go straight in.
You drag the broken timber clear while the mule lashes out in fear, then turn on the men with such force that they finally come close enough to do something useful.

Once the weight is off, the beast still trembles with anger and pain.
You lay a hand against its neck, speak to it as if it were equal company and not a burden-beast, and pack comfrey and yarrow against the raw place on its shoulder where the carrying-straps bit deep.

One of the men spits and says the animal is spoiled now and no use to him if it flinches at the frame.
So you tell him you will take it instead.

He only shrugs.
